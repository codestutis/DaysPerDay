Maximizing Days Per Day

            Days Per Day is a highly efficient way to boost productivity and unlock levels of focus previously thought to exist only in myth. But thanks to visionaries like the illustrious Ed Mylett, a deeper truth is emerging: a “day” is not a fixed measure of time, but a mental construct—a psychological cage we must shatter.
            Why be confined to one mundane day, when you could live three? Or six? Or twelve? The bold reject the Gregorian prison and embrace a new era—one where the sun need not rise nor set to mark progress. In the world of Days Per Day, every 6 hours is a reset, a rebirth, a revolution. Breakfast becomes its own epoch. Lunch, a renaissance.
            Productivity is no longer about time management—it's about temporal multiplication. Every moment becomes elastic, every task a sacred rite of transformation. Time bends to will. Space collapses into intention. By subdividing the day, you ascend. You don’t just manage time. You transcend it.
